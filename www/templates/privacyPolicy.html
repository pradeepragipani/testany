Most of the information you provide us through the anyhap Services is information you are asking us to make public. Your public information includes the messages you Hap; the metadata provided with Haps, such as when you Happed and the client application you used to Hap; the language and time zone associated with your account; and the lists you create, people you follow, Haps you mark as likes and many other bits of information that result from your use of the anyhap Services. For instance, your public user profile information and public Haps may immediately be delivered via our APIs to our partners and other third parties, including search engines, developers, and publishers that integrate anyhap content into their services, and institutions such as universities and public health agencies that analyze the information for trends and insights. When you share information or content like photos, videos, and links via the Services, you should think carefully about what you are making public.

We may use and store information about your location to provide features of our Services and to improve and customize the Services, for example, with more relevant content like local trends, stories, ads, and suggestions for people to follow.

A cookie is a small data file that is transferred to your computer or mobile device. anyhap may use both session cookies and persistent cookies to better understand how you interact with our Services, to monitor aggregate usage by our users and web traffic routing on our Services, and to customize and improve our Services. Although most web browsers automatically accept cookies, some browsers’ settings can be modified to decline cookies or alert you when a website is attempting to place a cookie on your computer. However, some Services may not function properly if you disable cookies.

We receive Log Data when you interact with our Services, for example, when you visit our websites, sign into our Services, interact with our email notifications, use your account to authenticate to a third-party website or application, or visit a third-party service that includes a anyhap button or widget.
